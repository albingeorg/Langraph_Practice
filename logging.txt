**AI stands for Artificial Intelligence.**

In its simplest form, AI refers to the development of computer systems that can perform tasks that would normally require human intelligence.

Think of it as training computers to "think" and "learn" in ways similar to how humans do, but often on a much larger scale and faster.

Here's a breakdown of what that generally means:

1.  **Learning:** AI systems can learn from data. Just like you learn from experience, AI algorithms are fed vast amounts of information (data) and learn to identify patterns, make predictions, and improve their performance over time without being explicitly programmed for every single scenario. This is often called **Machine Learning**.

2.  **Reasoning & Problem Solving:** AI can process information and make decisions or solve problems based on that information. For example, a chess AI can analyze the board and choose the best move.

3.  **Perception:** AI can interpret sensory input from the world.
    *   **Computer Vision:** Allows AI to "see" and understand images and videos (e.g., identifying objects, faces, reading text).
    *   **Natural Language Processing (NLP):** Allows AI to "hear" and "understand" human language, both spoken and written (e.g., voice assistants, translation tools).

4.  **Action:** Based on their learning, reasoning, and perception, AI systems can then take actions, whether it's giving you a recommendation, driving a car, or controlling a robot.

**Where do you see AI in action?**

You interact with AI constantly, often without realizing it:

*   **Voice assistants:** Siri, Alexa, Google Assistant
*   **Recommendation systems:** What movies to watch on Netflix, products to buy on Amazon, music on Spotify.
*   **Spam filters** in your email.
*   **Self-driving cars and navigation apps** (like Google Maps).
*   **Facial recognition** on your phone.
*   **Chatbots** for customer service.
*   **Medical diagnosis** (helping doctors identify diseases).
*   **Fraud detection** in banking.

In essence, AI aims to create machines that can mimic and even surpass human cognitive functions, making systems smarter, more efficient, and capable of solving complex problems.
